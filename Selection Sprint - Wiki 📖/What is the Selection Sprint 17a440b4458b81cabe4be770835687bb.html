🏁Introduction

Get ready for an intense but incredibly fun three-week coding adventure at the Selection Sprint!

With daily tasks, exams, and teamwork, we're here to test your determination and help you grab a spot in our school. It's a double-sided test that allows you to try out our self-learning methodology and discover your love for programming alongside your awesome peers. We'll challenge you with mind-blowing tasks to ensure that our school is filled with top-notch, self-driven students. This methodology is definitely not for everyone, but those who join us at kood/Jõhvi after a successful Sprint, will see that it’s a perfect fit!

During these action-packed weeks, the pace is fast, and you'll tackle a ton of tasks. Brace yourself, because some participants have even claimed they gained more practical programming knowledge in the Sprint than in an entire year at university! But hey, we don't want just anyone to make it through. Around 15-20% of candidates usually leave the process, but that's all part of the challenge. We're looking for the most motivated candidates who can handle the heat and excel in our program.

But wait, it's not all serious business! Alongside your like-minded peers, you'll learn to cooperate, support each other, and conquer tricky situations together. Or kood/Jõhvi team knows the importance of a well-rounded experience, so we'll organize awesome events to keep the communication flowing. Plus, who says you can't take the lead and start your own tradition? Let's kick off some football competitions, morning running groups, and more.

Looking back at our previous Selection Sprint, students can't help but gush about the incredible time they had. We're not just hyping it up – it truly became one of the best periods of their lives. Lifelong connections, lasting friendships, unforgettable moments, and who knows, maybe even a bit of romance? With us, you never know what's going to happen. So buckle up and get ready for a thrilling coding journey filled with excitement, growth, and memories you'll cherish forever!

A lot of talk but no clear facts... what’s actually happening?

Okay, yes, a lot of text and nothing clear. Well here you go, let’s get this out in black and white.

3 weeks = 2 weeks on-site + 1 week remote. Why? We want you to experience the school not only from the on-site but from a remote state as well. This way you have some expectations before making a decision to work and study together or stay on-site vs off-site for the school period.

Everyday topics. Series of 5-15 tasks around a particular notion, to be done over the course of a day during the Selection Sprint. All the tasks are in Java or using the terminal.

Weekly exams on Friday. Closed book exams with no usage of the internet to see how you have developed during your Selection Sprint period. NB! There are no mandatory exams during the actual school program.

Group Tasks on weekends. A group project during the weekend, where participants develop their problem-solving skills and co-creativity around a common problem. NB! There is no group task on the last weekend.

📍The Selection Sprint Overview

Objective

Introduction to command line interface and programming through Java language.

Glossary

A Topic is a series of exercises during the Selection Sprint
The Selection Sprint has 10 topics. Each topic consists of 5 to 20 exercises of increasing complexity that are meant to be completed in a fixed amount of time, usually 1 or 2 days.

Exams are a series of short algorithmic exercises of increasing difficulty, picked at random in a pool of exercises. They allow students to understand how well they have assimilated content learned up to that point.
Exams are scheduled every week on Fridays during the Selection Sprint on-site weeks and they last 4 hours. Exams are pass/fail, and students get points based on how well they performed (how many tasks passed all the tests).

A task is a basic unit block for topics and exams. Exercises consist of instructions, a list of functions to use to complete the exercise, and a program that can be used to test the code.

Group Tasks are group exercises that are done over the weekend during the Sprints, over a 48 to 72-hour period. Their objective is to consolidate the learning outcomes from the previous week.
Groups are created randomly from the pool of registered students.

Points - students gain points for each exercise they successfully complete in the platform (tasks, topics, exams, …). Some exercises have time limitations and sprinters may not get the corresponding points if they do not complete the exercise in the allocated time. Points serve as an indicator of difficulty and overall progress, the harder the task the higher the Point reward.
The Points calculation is dynamic and is a function of various factors such as type of object, difficulty, allocated time, and group size (for group projects).
Sprinters can see how many Points they have.

Components

10 Topics, 2 Exams, 2 Group Tasks, 1 Dummy Exam

Duration

3 weeks

From Monday of week #1 to Friday of week #3

Full-time including weekends (2 weeks on-site and 1 week remotely)

P.S. The last weekend of the Selection Sprint will be meant for you to finish everything that needs to be finished, there won’t be any additional tasks.
